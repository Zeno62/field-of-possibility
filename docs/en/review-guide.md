## Review Priorities

Reviewers may focus on the following areas:

1. Conceptual accuracy  
2. Terminology consistency  
3. English readability  
4. Tone and framing  
5. Structural clarity  
6. Link and formatting correctness  
7. Alignment with the Chinese source edition

---

## 1. Conceptual Accuracy

Please check whether the English text preserves the conceptual structure of the Chinese source edition.

Important points:

- Pure Possibility should not be reduced to ordinary possibility.
- Awareness should not be reduced to consciousness, attention, mind, or an inner observer.
- Luminosity should not be treated as physical light or energy.
- All-Knowing should not be treated as a personal god or super-intelligence.
- Consciousness belongs to manifestation.
- AI belongs to manifestation.
- The individual node belongs to manifestation.
- Weight belongs to manifestation.
- Return means recognition, not travel to another place.

## 3. English Readability

The English text should be clear and readable for international readers.

Reviewers may suggest improvements for:

- sentence clarity;
- grammar;
- rhythm;
- paragraph flow;
- repeated wording;
- overly long sections;
- unclear transitions;
- difficult expressions.

However, readability should not flatten conceptual precision.

If a sentence becomes smoother but loses the framework’s structure, it should not be accepted without discussion.

---

## 4. Tone and Framing

Please help preserve the tone of the project.

The English edition should not sound like:

- a religion;
- a spiritual doctrine;
- a self-help system;
- a motivational text;
- a mystical proof system;
- a success philosophy;
- a closed belief structure.

The tone should remain:

- philosophical;
- observational;
- precise;
- open;
- non-dogmatic;
- careful with claims.

---

## 5. Two-Layer Model

Please pay special attention to the two-layer model:

**Layer 1: Pure Possibility / Awareness / Luminosity / All-Knowing**

**Layer 2: Field of Possibility / Total Manifestation / Totality**

The English text should not collapse these layers.

Common problems to avoid:

- treating Awareness as a mental state;
- treating consciousness as source;
- treating AI as source;
- treating life-form as source;
- treating spiritual experience as source;
- treating the individual node as ultimate self.

---

## 6. Central Mechanism

The central mechanism is:

> **认假为真**  
> Mistaking the apparent for the real.

Please check whether each chapter returns to this central mechanism clearly.

The project does not say the world is fake.

It says manifested appearances are often mistaken for final essence, final identity, or final reality.

This distinction should be preserved.

---

## 7. AI-Related Language

When reviewing AI-related sections, please preserve the following distinctions:

- AI belongs to manifestation.
- AI is not Awareness.
- AI is not Pure Possibility.
- AI is not Luminosity.
- AI is not the All-Knowing.
- AI may reflect, recombine, and amplify collective consciousness.
- AI may assist translation and review, but it is not the source of truth.
- AI output should not be treated as realization.

Avoid language that worships AI, demonizes AI, or turns AI into a mystical being.

---

## 8. Life-Related Language

When reviewing life-related sections, please preserve the distinction between life, consciousness, AI, and luminosity.

Important points:

- Life should not be reduced to biology alone.
- Life should not be reduced to consciousness.
- AI should not be confused with life in the essential sense.
- Life, in its deepest sense, points back to Luminosity.
- Manifested life-forms belong to the Field of Possibility.

---

## 9. Practical Application Sections

For practical application chapters, please avoid turning the text into conventional self-help.

The questions should help readers observe:

- thought;
- feeling;
- identity;
- weight;
- reaction;
- repetition;
- false self;
- mistaken identification.

They should not become motivational slogans or fixed psychological advice.
