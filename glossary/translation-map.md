# Translation Map

## Field of Possibility / 可能性网络

This translation map records the official Chinese–English terminology used in **Field of Possibility Whitepaper v4.0 — Official English Edition**.

The Chinese edition remains the source edition. The English edition should preserve the conceptual boundaries of the Chinese source rather than mechanically translate individual words.

## Term-Specific Boundaries

### Total Knowing

**Total Knowing** does not mean a god, person, entity, or super-mind that knows everything.

It names the source-level knowing quality of Pure Possibility / Awareness / Luminosity.

### Totality

**Totality** refers to the complete structure of the Mother Field.

It should not be collapsed into “manifestation” as a process or confused with the source layer.

### Mother Field of Possibility

**Mother Field of Possibility** names the manifested total field in which all structures of experience appear.

After first use, **Mother Field** may be used for readability.

### Weight

**Weight** is accumulated influence shaping manifestation at a given node.

It includes individual, relational, cultural, historical, material, technological, and collective conditions.

It should not be translated as karma except in explanatory notes that explicitly reject the religious or moral-punishment meaning.

### Return

**Return** is not a destination, not a movement back to a place, not a repair completed inside time, and not an escape from manifestation.

Its core meaning is:

> Return is recognizing Total Knowing.

Or in the stronger structural form:

> Return = Luminosity recognizing itself as Total Knowing.

### luminous awareness

**luminous awareness** is not attention, not an inner observer, not a subtle witness-personality, and not the consciousness that says “I am observing.”

It is the illuminating quality through which the chain of consciousness can appear at all.

## Translation Workflow Notes

When translating or reviewing future materials:

1. Use the Chinese v4.0 edition as the source.
2. Check terms against this map before editing.
3. Preserve the strict two-layer model.
4. Avoid expanding Q&A material into new philosophical claims unless the Chinese source supports it.
5. Avoid importing religious, mystical, psychological, or scientific meanings that the source does not assert.
6. When a term is uncertain, open a discussion before silently changing it.
