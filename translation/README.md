## Translation Principles

Translations should follow these principles:

- preserve the Chinese edition as the source edition;
- translate concepts carefully rather than mechanically;
- protect the strict two-layer model;
- maintain the distinction between Awareness and consciousness;
- maintain the distinction between Pure Possibility and ordinary possibility;
- avoid turning the framework into religion, self-help, or mystical doctrine;
- avoid making scientific claims that the source text does not make;
- preserve conceptual precision over literary fluency when necessary;
- mark uncertain terms for discussion rather than silently changing them.

## Translation Status Labels

Each translated chapter may use one of the following labels:

- **draft** — initial translation or adaptation, not yet reviewed;
- **reviewed** — reviewed by contributors, but not yet final;
- **official** — accepted as an official translation;
- **outdated** — source version has changed and the translation needs updating.

## Suggested Review Process

For each translated chapter, reviewers may check:

1. **Conceptual accuracy**

   Does the translation preserve the meaning of the Chinese source?

2. **Terminology consistency**

   Are key terms translated consistently with the translation map?

3. **Two-layer clarity**

   Does the translation preserve the distinction between source layer and manifestation layer?

4. **Tone**

   Does the text avoid becoming religious, self-help, motivational, or mystical in a vague way?

5. **Readability**

   Is the English readable for international readers without flattening the concepts?

6. **Structural clarity**

   Are sections clear enough for readers to follow the framework step by step?

## AI-Assisted Translation

AI tools may be used to assist translation, editing, comparison, and terminology review.

However:

- AI output should not be treated as final authority;
- AI may flatten or spiritualize terms;
- AI may introduce familiar but inaccurate language;
- AI may miss distinctions that are central to the framework;
- human review remains necessary.

AI can assist the translation process, but the Chinese source edition remains the source of truth.
